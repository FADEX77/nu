About Bowen University

Bowen University is a private Baptist Christian university located in Iwo, Osun State, Nigeria. Established in 2001 by the Nigerian Baptist Convention, the university is named after Rev. Thomas Jefferson Bowen, who pioneered Baptist work in Nigeria in 1850.

The university is committed to providing quality education within a Christian environment, preparing students for leadership roles in various sectors of society. Bowen University combines academic excellence with moral and spiritual values, aiming to produce graduates who are not only knowledgeable but also of good character.

The institution has grown significantly over the years and now offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various disciplines. Bowen University is known for its serene environment, conducive for learning, and its emphasis on discipline and character development.

Admission Requirements

Undergraduate Programs

  • Five O'Level credits including English Language and Mathematics
  • Acceptable UTME score (180 and above)
  • Post-UTME screening
  • Direct Entry candidates must have relevant A'Level or equivalent qualifications

Postgraduate Programs

  • Bachelor's degree with at least Second Class Lower division
  • NYSC discharge or exemption certificate
  • Relevant professional qualifications (where applicable)
  • Entrance examination and/or interview

Application Process
