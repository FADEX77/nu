About Babcock University

Babcock University is a private Christian co-educational Nigerian university owned and operated by the Seventh-day Adventist Church. It is one of the most prestigious private universities in Nigeria, known for its excellence in academics, research, and character development.

The university was named after David Babcock, an American missionary who pioneered the Seventh-day Adventist work in Nigeria. What began as the Adventist College of West Africa (ACWA) in 1959 was later renamed Adventist Seminary of West Africa (ASWA) in 1975. In 1999, it was granted a university charter by the Federal Government of Nigeria.

Babcock University has grown to become a center of excellence in education, offering a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various disciplines. The university is particularly renowned for its medical school, law faculty, and business programs.

Admission Requirements

Undergraduate Programs

  • Five O'Level credits including English Language and Mathematics
  • Acceptable UTME score (180 and above)
  • Post-UTME screening
  • Direct Entry candidates must have relevant A'Level or equivalent qualifications

Postgraduate Programs

  • Bachelor's degree with at least Second Class Lower division
  • NYSC discharge or exemption certificate
  • Relevant professional qualifications (where applicable)
  • Entrance examination and/or interview
