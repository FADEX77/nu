About Benson Idahosa University

Benson Idahosa University (BIU) is a private Christian university located in Benin City, Edo State, Nigeria. Established in 2002 by the Church of God Mission International, the university is named after Archbishop Benson Idahosa, a renowned Nigerian Christian leader and founder of the Church of God Mission International.

The university was founded with the vision of raising leaders who are spiritually sound, intellectually sharp, and who will impact their generation positively. BIU is committed to providing quality education within a Christian environment, preparing students for leadership roles in various sectors of society.

The institution combines academic excellence with moral and spiritual values, aiming to produce graduates who are not only knowledgeable but also of good character. Benson Idahosa University is known for its serene environment, conducive for learning, and its emphasis on entrepreneurship and practical skills development.

Admission Requirements

Undergraduate Programs

  • Five O'Level credits including English Language and Mathematics
  • Acceptable UTME score (180 and above)
  • Post-UTME screening
  • Direct Entry candidates must have relevant A'Level or equivalent qualifications

Postgraduate Programs

  • Bachelor's degree with at least Second Class Lower division
  • NYSC discharge or exemption certificate
  • Relevant professional qualifications (where applicable)
  • Entrance examination and/or interview

Application Process
